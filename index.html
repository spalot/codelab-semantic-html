Google Picks Former Obama Adviser to Lead Global Public Policy

Google, facing increased scrutiny from European regulators, has hired a former senior adviser to President Obama to lead its global public policy team. Caroline Atkinson, who left her position as a White House deputy national security adviser last month, will join Google in March and be based in Washington. Her most pressing task will be to temper concerns by antitrust enforcement officials in the European Union, which has accused the company of abusing its dominance in web search.
